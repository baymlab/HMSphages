# 2. Acquiring and processing environmental samples

## Objective
To collect samples from the environment that may contain phages that infect your host bacteria

## Rationale
By collecting diverse environmental samples of soil, water, compost etc. you will aim to capture new phages. The golden rule in phage hunting is *"where you find your host is where you will find its phages"*, so bear in mind that environments that your specific host bacteria thrive in are where you are most likely to find phages. Secondly, the more diverse samples you can collect, the higher your chances of finding something. Phages are so small that just a tablespoon of soil could contain thousands of different phages, so itâ€™s less important to take large volumes of sample, and more important to get a diversity of different samples.
